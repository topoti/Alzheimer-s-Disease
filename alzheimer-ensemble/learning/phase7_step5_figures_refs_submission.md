# Phase 7 · Step 5 — Figures, References, Review & Submission

> **Phase 7 — Paper Writing** (closes Phase 7)
> The goal of this step: polish the manuscript to submission quality and send it to the right venue.

The one question this step answers:

> **How do I get from a complete draft to a submitted, defensible paper?**

---

## 1. Figures — make them a coherent set

- One **consistent style** across all figures (same `seaborn` theme, fonts, colormap). Mismatched figures read as sloppy.
- Every figure: high resolution (vector/PDF where possible), readable axis labels, a self-contained caption.
- The set: preprocessing pipeline, architecture, ensemble fusion (Phase 7 Step 3); confusion matrix, ROC curves (Phase 6 Step 2); the 4-column XAI figure (Phase 5 Step 4); class-distribution + before/after-ADASYN charts (Phase 3).

---

## 2. References — final pass

- 35–55 references, all through Zotero/Mendeley in the journal's style.
- Every in-text citation resolves; every reference is cited. No dead entries.
- Primary sources present: ADASYN, Grad-CAM, SHAP, LIME, ResNet, EfficientNet, DenseNet, and Mahmud et al. (2024).

---

## 3. Review — three sets of eyes

1. **Advisor** — technical correctness and framing.
2. **Two peers** — clarity and flow.
3. **The "stranger read"** (the plan's Phase 7 verification) — give it to someone *outside your field*; if they can summarize your **three contributions in one sentence**, the framing works. If not, revise the Intro.

Then run a **plagiarism check** (Turnitin / iThenticate via your institution) before submission.

---

## 4. Venue — submit smart

| Journal | Publisher | ~IF | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diagnostics** (MDPI) | MDPI | ~3.6 | Same venue as the reference paper — reviewers know the comparison. **Submit here first.** |
| Scientific Reports | Nature | ~4.0 | Broad, fast — good fallback |
| PeerJ Computer Science | PeerJ | ~3.5 | Open-access, beginner-friendly fallback |
| Computers in Biology and Medicine | Elsevier | ~7.0 | Higher prestige, longer review |
| IEEE JBHI | IEEE | ~7.7 | Strong methodology bar |
| Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience | Frontiers | ~4.8 | Clinical angle |

**Recommendation:** **Diagnostics** first (direct comparison familiarity); fall back to **Scientific Reports** or **PeerJ CS**.

### Resources (a few hours total)
- Target journal's **submission checklist** and **author guidelines** (figure formats, word limits, ethics/data-availability statements).
- Your institution's iThenticate/Turnitin access.

---
